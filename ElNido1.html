Top Mainland Attractions

Nacpan Beach

Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer stretch of powdery cream-colored sand lined with swaying coconut trees and clear turquoise waters, located about 17 kilometers north of El Nido town in Palawan Island. Once a hidden gem, Nacpan Beach has grown into one of the top attractions in El Nido Town, attracting beach lovers in search of quiet, unspoiled shores. It’s still relatively undeveloped compared to other famous beaches in the Philippines, offering a rustic charm perfect for relaxing, swimming, and watching the sunset. Visitors can spend the day sunbathing, renting a kayak or paddleboard, or enjoying a quiet lunch at one of the beachside eateries.

Las Cabanas Beach

Las Cabañas Beach, located in El Nido, Palawan, is one of the most popular spots for relaxation and breathtaking sunsets. The beach is known for its clean, turquoise waters, soft sand, and scenic surroundings that include limestone cliffs and a view of nearby islands. The atmosphere is laid-back, with several beachfront bars and restaurants offering visitors a place to relax, enjoy cocktails, and soak in the sun.

Activities to do

  • Las Cabanas Zipline
  • Sunset Watching
  • Beach Hopping & Sunbathing
  • Water Sports
  • each Bars and Dining
  • Massages

Taraw Peak

Taraw Cliff, located in El Nido, Palawan, Philippines, is one of the most iconic landmarks in the region, offering panoramic views of Bacuit Bay and its karst limestone formations. It’s up there with my favorite viewpoints in the Philippines. It may only be a 30-minute journey to the summit but it’s also incredibly tough with extreme humidity and sharp rocks.

Activities to do

  • Sunrise Hiking
  • Via Ferrata Canopy Walk
  • Photography
  • Hiking & Climbing

Nagkalit-Kalit Waterfalls

Whispered about by the rustling palms and the gentle waves of El Nido, Nagkalit-Kalit Falls is one of Palawan's hidden treasures. This cascading beauty is nestled amidst the verdant jungle, offering adventurers a chance to immerse themselves in the island’s natural splendor. It is not just a single waterfall, but a series of small falls, each pouring into the next, culminating in a refreshing pool at the base—perfect for a cooling dip after a trek through the jungle.
